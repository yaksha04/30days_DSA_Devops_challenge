1. Foundations: OSI Model vs. TCP/IPThink of these as the "rules of the road" for data.OSI Model (7 Layers): A theoretical framework. In DevOps, you’ll spend 90% of your time at:Layer 4 (Transport): Ports, TCP/UDP. (e.g., Load balancing at the port level).Layer 7 (Application): HTTP, DNS, TLS. (e.g., Ingress controllers, WAFs).TCP/IP Model: The practical implementation used by the internet.TCP (Transmission Control Protocol): Reliable, connection-oriented (3-way handshake). Used for web traffic and databases.UDP (User Datagram Protocol): Fast, connectionless. Used for streaming and DNS.2. DNS: The Internet's PhonebookDNS translates human-readable names (google.com) into machine-readable IP addresses.A Record: Maps a name to an IPv4 address.CNAME: Maps a name to another name (useful for pointing a domain to a Load Balancer's DNS name).TTL (Time to Live): How long a record is cached. In migrations, you lower this to propagate changes faster.3. Load Balancing & Reverse ProxyThese two are often confused, but they serve different roles in a pipeline.Load Balancer (LB)Distributes incoming traffic across multiple backend servers to ensure high availability.L4 LB: Routes based on IP and Port (faster, lower overhead).L7 LB: Routes based on URL paths, cookies, or headers (more intelligent).Reverse ProxyActs as a "front door" for your application.Core functions: SSL Termination (handling HTTPS so your app doesn't have to), Caching, and Anonymizing your backend servers.Key Tools: Nginx, HAProxy, Traefik.4. VPC & Subnetting (The Cloud Network)A VPC (Virtual Private Cloud) is your private corner of the cloud.Subnets: Segments of your VPC.Public Subnet: Has a route to the Internet Gateway (e.g., for Load Balancers).Private Subnet: No direct internet access (e.g., for Databases/Internal Apps).CIDR Notation: How we define IP ranges.Example: 10.0.0.0/16 gives you 65,536 IPs. A smaller /24 gives you 256 IPs.5. NAT (Network Address Translation)NAT allows instances in a Private Subnet to talk to the internet (to download patches or updates) without allowing the internet to initiate a connection back to them.NAT Gateway: A managed service in AWS/GCP that handles this translation.Security Benefit: It keeps your database servers hidden from the public web while still allowing them to pull software updates.6.
